IOT SECURITY

If scale and complexity are significant contributors to mobile security risk, then the Internet of Things (IOT) multiplies that risk a thousand-fold. As the IoT ramps up, so does the need to secure and manage the endpoints. Experts forecast that over 23 billion connected 'Things' will be in use by 2018, trippling to more than 75 billion by 2025. That's 75 billion endpoints delivering essential functionality at the edge of your network - sensors, actuators, printers, scanners, wearables and robots, as well as a many more 'things' that we don't even know about yet.

Historically, IoT devices have had poor security and are attractive targets for cyber criminals. In late 2016, hackers compromised thousands of poorly secured IoT endpoints to create a botnet that executed a massic DDoS attack on a key part of the internet infrastructure. This was only the first of many large cyber attacks that used IoT devices as the attack vector.



PREVENTION / MITIGATION

Full Lifecycle Management Because they are at the edge of your network and often unaattended, IoT endpoints need to be used secured just as much as enterprise mobile devices, if not more so. Having visibility into these endpints in your EMM solution will give you the ability to secure and manage them throughout their full lifecycle, from deployment to retirement.
Strong Password Pollicies Weak or unchanged default passwords are often exploited by malicious parties. Enforce complex password policies to prevent unauthorized parties from using manual/brute-force techniques to guess the passwords and gaining access to the device.
Patching/Updating OS and Apps An EMM solution can identify and segregate devices rnning old/vulnerable OSes/ app update. On IoT devices, this capability is critical as they often lack the device manufacturer, carrier or app store services that are used to update mobiles OSes and apps.
